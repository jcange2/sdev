Nettle
Urtica dioica

Nettle is often found in the wild and is known for its sting when touched. Once dried or cooked, it becomes safe to consume and is widely used in herbal traditions.

Identification Tip: Nettle has serrated, heart-shaped leaves and fine hairs that can cause a stinging sensation when touched.

Traditional Use: Traditionally, nettle has been used in teas and herbal preparations as a natural antihistamine support, and is also associated with supporting prostate and kidney health, along with general seasonal and joint wellness.

Nutrition: Nettle leaves are rich in vitamins A, C, and K, as well as iron, calcium, and magnesium.
